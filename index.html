California Fire Hazard Zones

The severity classification map for California delineates Fire Hazard Zones, where the classification is determined by an amalgamation of pivotal factors. These factors encompass historical fire data, the intricacies of the terrain, the prevalence of fire-prone weather patterns, and the abundance of potential fuel for fires, primarily in the form of natural vegetation. The state meticulously categorizes Hazard Severity Zones into three distinct levels providing a comprehensive framework for understanding and addressing the varying degrees of fire risk across the region.

  • High
