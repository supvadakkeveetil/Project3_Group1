Wildfires

Wildfires constitute a formidable threat to our landscapes, casting a pervasive impact that reverberates across multiple dimensions. Beyond the immediate danger to flora and fauna, these relentless infernos wield a profound influence on air quality, directly affecting the health and well-being of both human and animal life. The repercussions extend beyond the immediate timeframe, leaving enduring impacts that resonate across ecosystems, communities, and the broader environment.

California Wildfires

California's susceptibility to fire weather is deeply rooted in its Mediterranean Climate, characterized by seasonally dry conditions.

A closer look at California Wildfires (2000-2020)

The largest wildfire in California history is the August Complex Wildfire which occured in the year 2020. The fire was caused by Lightning.

California Fire Hazard Zones

The severity classification map for California delineates Fire Hazard Zones, where the classification is determined by an amalgamation of pivotal factors.
These factors encompass historical fire data, the intricacies of the terrain, the prevalence of fire-prone weather patterns, and the abundance of potential fuel for fires, primarily in the form of natural vegetation.
The state meticulously categorizes Hazard Severity Zones into three distinct levels.

  • Moderate
  • High
  • Very High
